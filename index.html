I am a graduate student in Data Science at The University of Hong Kong, building on an undergraduate background in Statistics with a minor in Computer Science from Shandong University. My research interests center on reliable and self-improving LLM agents — developing methods that help agentic systems learn from feedback and self-correct, while keeping that reliability measurable and verifiable over long horizons.

I am seeking research assistant opportunities as a step toward a PhD in these areas.

Research Interests

  • Reliable and self-improving LLM agents agents that improve through structured feedback over long horizons, where the hard part is credit assignment and error accumulation across many steps.
  • Feedback-based post-training and verifiable reward modeling RL and preference-optimization methods such as PPO, GRPO, and DPO, and process-level reward models that give dense, less hackable training signals beyond final-answer supervision — and the conditions under which such training improves held-out capability rather than only the training reward.
  • Synthetic data and environment construction for verifiable training synthesizing structured tasks, reasoning traces, and interactive environments that yield scalable, rule-based reward signals for agent post-training.
  • Causal and uncertainty-aware evaluation of LLM capabilities using causal inference to separate genuine capability from spurious correlations and benchmark artifacts, and treating benchmark scores as estimates with confidence intervals, not point estimates alone.

Research

EM and MCEM for Parameter Estimation in Regression Models B.S. Thesis · May 2025

Derived and implemented EM/MCEM with multicycle-ECM for Student's t-regression and linear mixed models in R, including REML corrections that remove the small-sample downward bias in variance components. Validated every estimator to machine precision against lme4, nlme, and optim over 500-replication simulations, and applied the methods to robust facial-age regression and a 60k-observation hierarchical reaction-time model.

Selected Projects

Served as the project lead and primary contributor on every team project, with several others completed independently as the sole author.

Meeting Agent — Multimodal RAG with Long-Term Memory Project Lead · Apr 2026

Architected the core RAG retrieval funnel — scope routing (corpus → meeting → file), hybrid dense+sparse recall (Chroma + BM25/FTS5), Reciprocal Rank Fusion with per-file fair allocation, anchor-based eviction for session continuity, and Cohere/BGE reranking — yielding citation-grounded, traceable answers. Added a content-aware ingestion cascade with speaker-diarized ASR, plus a long-term memory layer with TTL/importance-decayed facts, a knowledge graph of entities and relations, and episodic cross-session summaries. Built with FastAPI and LangChain LCEL.

Zero-Shot CNNs from Textual Descriptions — Reproduction & Extension Project Lead · Mar 2026

Reproduced Ba et al. (ICCV 2015) in PyTorch — predicting convolutional and fully-connected classifier weights directly from Wikipedia text to recognize unseen classes on CUB-200-2011 and Oxford Flowers (~0.71 unseen ROC-AUC), with the residual gap from the paper traced mainly to its now-unavailable CUB-200-2010 benchmark. Extended it with auxiliary losses (CLIP contrastive, center-alignment), modern text encoders (SBERT, CLIP), and backbones (DenseNet-121, ResNet-50) — the strongest, a CLIP text encoder, reaching 0.89 unseen ROC-AUC.

Web-Agent — Autonomous VLM-Driven Browser Agent Project Lead · Nov 2025

Built an autonomous agent that carries out natural-language web tasks by fusing a vision-language model's screenshot with a DOM-to-Markdown snapshot to decide the next Playwright action. On the STAT7008A (HKU) team project I led, I implemented the local/API dual-mode vLLM planner and document image extraction, and independently simplified the browser-use library. My solo post-course rewrite swapped the local-only stack for a provider-agnostic planner (with automatic vision detection), a quality-gated OCR cascade (Marker→MinerU→PaddleOCR→PyMuPDF) that resolves a figure by its real caption, and four-signal loop detection. Typed, protocol-based Python package (~13.5k LOC, 50+ tools, 186 tests).

NLLB-200 Chinese→English Translation — LoRA Fine-Tuning Personal · Nov 2025

LoRA-fine-tuned Meta's NLLB-200-distilled (1.3B) for Chinese→English translation on 600k WMT19 sentence pairs, adapting only low-rank updates (r=32) on the attention and feed-forward projections — trained in ~5.5 hours on a single 16 GB GPU with bfloat16 and gradient accumulation. Built a multi-stage cleaning pipeline (normalization, length/ratio filtering, language-consistency checks, exact-pair deduplication) and evaluated with sacreBLEU under 5-beam search, reaching 24.9 BLEU on the WMT19 test split.

CIFAR-100 Image Classification — WideResNet from Scratch Personal · Oct 2025

Built a WideResNet-28-10 (~36.5M parameters) from scratch in PyTorch and pushed it to 0.832 macro-F1 across 100 classes. Designed a two-tier augmentation strategy — semantic-aware offline transforms routed by class/superclass with difficulty-scaled intensity and confusion-pair awareness, plus online AutoAugment, RandomErasing, MixUp, and CutMix — combined with label smoothing, weight EMA, SGD+Nesterov, and a warmup→cosine schedule, training in ~2.5 hours on one RTX 4080 Super.

ViT Image-to-LaTeX — Math Expression Recognition Personal · Jun 2025

Built a pure Vision Transformer encoder–decoder from scratch in PyTorch — no CNN backbone or grammar rules (~7.7M parameters) — mapping formula images to LaTeX token sequences via patch embeddings and an autoregressive decoder. Trained and evaluated on both printed (im2latex-100k) and handwritten (MathWriting) corpora, reaching 90.1% token accuracy and 0.70 BLEU-4 (beam search) on printed im2latex-100k. Studied the training dynamics in depth — finding gradient clipping essential for stability, that teacher-forced token accuracy substantially overstates true autoregressive generation quality, and that handwritten recognition remains markedly harder than printed.

CNN, BiLSTM & Transformer for Text Classification Personal · May 2025

Derived all three architectures from first principles — the recurrent family (S-RNN → LSTM → Peephole → BiLSTM) with gating and BPTT derivations, CNN inductive biases, and multi-head self-attention — then benchmarked them on four datasets (IMDB, AG News, BBC News, SST-5), tying the measured O(n²) throughput drop to a complexity analysis and showing that architecture–task fit outweighs model capacity.

Library Management System — Database & Full-Stack Personal · 2022–2023

Two implementations of the same domain: a SQL Server command-line system built around stored procedures, views, and triggers for reader and administrator roles, then a full-stack web rebuild with a Flask REST API, a Vue 2 + Element UI frontend, and a MySQL backend — emphasizing relational schema design and separated frontend/backend architecture.
